Digital Soil

Welcome to Digital Soil! As the name implies, we are digitizing soil and everything that goes with it. We all know that watering plants and gathering data now can be done with simple devices called (IoT) Internet of Things. The idea is to represent reality with today's technology with today's field data gathering and workforce management.

Open source platform dedicated to field of agriculture. We are focused on representing real live data and process from Compost, Growing, and maintenance of your plants, equipment and farm. Our goal is to provide a web base application where all your data can be utilized in your favor. Let the machine do your automated tasks and provide you with useful data that will be implemented through all API connections with other Internet of Things Providers and Services. This will be your home base preference manager for all your Internet of Things and Field Work managing web site.

With the help of Soil FoodWeb Alliance we are know able to provide a source where a professional may view and post opinions and recommendations on your compost pile and turning cycles.

Our goal is to provide a web service implementing Internet of Things, so field work and field sensors can integrate into our service, providing a place where the data gathered will work for you. Providing resorces for desition making and automatization of desired events.

Sensors can be implemented no matter your service provider, as long as they have API services.

Soil FoodWeb Alliance

Soil contains one of the most diverse habitats on earth. A single teaspoon of soil may contain millions of individual organisms and tens of thousands of species of microbes: bacteria, fungi, protozoa, nematodes, arthropods … on up to earthworms, ants, spiders, and voles.

The more biodiversity contained within the soil, the healthier and more resilient the plants (and the animals that eat the plants) will be. Unfortunately, modern agricultural practices have abused our soil to the point where it can no longer support or sustain a healthy and diverse array of soil “critters”. Compost can return the nutrients absent in a lifeless soil, and support the organisms which will continue to build the soil for years to come.
