Who We are Soil FoodWeb Alliance, Founded in 2016, the Soil Food Web Alliance is an organization and a grassroots movement that teaches how soil and soil biology can enhance ecological, societal, and even personal health.  The Soil Food Web Alliance works to strengthen the relationship we as humans have with soil, by teaching practical soil stewardship skills that have personal and non-personal applications.  We work to reunite humans with the soil and to help all remember the importance soil has played in our biological and social evolution.

As a philosophy, the Soil Food Web Alliance suggests that the highly resilient life cycles of soil biology and microbiology and their interactions in nature serve as a powerful learning tool for how humans can best relate to each other and help steward the environment.  We believe that many unique lessons can be gleaned from observing and interacting with soil microbiology and we work to communicate those insights in hopes of inspiring others to do the same.

Our goalsover the comming years is to continue building a thriving community of soil stewards around the world, building a strong network of folks eager to engage soil in the design of ever-healthier ecologies.  We believe our movement can succeed with strong communication, sustainable practices, and, most importantly, widespread collaboration.

Our Mission

Our mission is to raise awareness about the importance of soil biology in the interest of nature and land preservation

Our Goals

  • To build an ecologically-conscious, solutions-oriented, and non-discriminatory grassroots movement of applied soil stewards.
  • Increase soil microbial diversity, carbon sequestration, water quality, and soil health in disturbed habitats through the dissemination of applied soil regenerative practices.

Our current efforts to accomplish these goals center on the development of this website into an information database and networking hub.

Why Soil Biology?

We live in a unique era were the human connection to soil is becoming stronger, one could even argue that for the first time in history, a clear awareness of the profound ecological importance of soil biology which is paired with a consistent, accessible, and inexpensive techniques for cultivating these organisms.

Soil biology (macro and micro) are undeniably the central importance to all ecological cycles. Innovative research in applied soil microbiology continues to demonstrate that soil microorganisms offer a unique solution to a range of environmental issues.

We at Soil Food Web Alliance believe in the power of our microbe allies to help humans address many global challenges, but only if the awareness is built around this forgotten aspect of nature. Throughout the natural world microorganisms act as primary regenerators of life and we see our work with soil and soil biology as a unique means for learning from these organisms in the search for new paradigms for regenerative human-ecological relations.

The SWFA Volunteer Network

The SFWA Network is an international, all-volunteer, grassroots network of groups and individuals that share information related to soil biology and ecology, soil health, and soil regeneration.

PRINCIPLES

Members of the SFWA Network support their local communities by increasing access to information regarding soil biology and ecology, for people of all backgrounds through the open dissemination of relevant information in a manner that is egalitarian, all-inclusive, and does not discriminate on the basis of age, gender, ethnicity, religion, mental health status, race, culture, sexual orientation, general appearance, income level, living situation, or experience.

ORGANIZATION

SFWA Groups make relatively autonomous decisions around how to work in their local environment while still being connected to and supported by the larger SFW movement. SFWA Groups work autonomously in their local region but are also networked together and supported in part through an online format. How often SFWA Groups meet is an internal decision made by the group’s own members. The SFWA Collective is a small group that works to support the SFWA Network. Branches help the SFWA Collective develop a SFWA movement by providing suggestions on how to best evolve The Network.

As the SFWA Network continues to expand, working groups will be established to help identify and disseminate tasks among Network members that help support the SFWA movement. These working groups are open to Network contacts and chapters that wish to plug in deeper and help get work done.


How to Help

Our mission is to raise awareness around the importance of soil biology and microbiology for increasing the health of humans and the environment.

We achieve this mission through the production of new ‘soil food web-focused’ media. We make videos, hold interviews, write instructional literature, hold workshops, organize internationally-attended events, and offer a range of visual learning tools and platforms. Our programs and projects strive to meet various needs, as well as to create stronger connections and community among current and future generations of soil stewards. We strive towards a soil-stewardship culture that is holistic and sustainable, and we emphasize appropriate technologies, open community engagement, and environmental advocacy in all of our work – traits that distinguish our work and help characterize the advocates, practitioners, and researchers we serve and work with. 

Our ongoing and upcoming projects depend on support to continue building on the momentum we have built over the years. If you feel our work is of service to you and your community, please consider donating through our PayPal account. 

Other ways to support and get involved:

  • Join the SFWA Network and become an active collaborator
  • Invite us to present a talk or workshop at your school, organization, or event.
  • Print and distribute our publication (we don’t currently have this)
  • Help us translate our publication into different languages.
  • Like and follow us on social media.
  • Contact us with feedback, suggestions for collaboration, or anything else you with to share.
